# Q1. Introductions — who are you and what do you do?

I'm Xavier Lee, co-founder of Jumpstart Commerce. We're a Shopify and CRM agency based in Singapore, and we've been a Klaviyo Platinum Partner since 2022. Most of our work sits in Southeast Asia — we run email, WhatsApp, and Meta for high-growth ecommerce brands across the region.

Omnidesk has been one of our key partnerships. They're a great example of what happens when a brand takes omnichannel seriously in this part of the world.

# Q2. How did you end up working together?

When we came into the Omnidesk account, the bones were there. They had data, decent email, a team that understood their customer. What wasn't there was the connection between channels. Flows were running but not really driving revenue. WhatsApp wasn't activated at all. Meta was living in its own world.

What we saw early was simple: if we could get one customer profile powering all three channels, the numbers would move fast. And they did.

*(~25s. The phrase "the bones were there" is the one to land — it sets up the whole rebuild narrative.)*

# Q3. What you see when a brand's been on the wrong platform

Every comeback account looks a little different, but the pattern is the same. Tired lists, broken flows, a team that's lost trust in their own data. The rebuild isn't just technical, it's psychological. You have to prove the platform works again before you can ask the brand to invest in it properly.

With Omnidesk, the data was in better shape than most comeback situations we see. So we could skip the salvage phase and go straight into strategy. That's a big part of why the restart happened so quickly.

*(~30s. This is Ben's question mostly — keep your part short, support the brand's story.)*

# Q4. What you see in brands without a strong omnichannel CRM foundation

Most brands in SEA are running channels, they just aren't running them together. Email in one tool, WhatsApp in another, Meta audiences uploaded from a spreadsheet once a week. Every channel has a different view of the customer, which means no channel has the full view.

What stood out about Omnidesk was that they already understood this was the problem. They didn't need convincing. They needed a partner who could fix it properly inside Klaviyo instead of stitching four tools together.

*(~30s.)*

---

# Q5. Approach to WhatsApp strategy for Omnidesk

Our approach was to treat WhatsApp as a revenue channel from day one, not a support channel with marketing bolted on. So the first decisions we made were about what we'd use it for, which segments we'd talk to, and how we'd measure success.

The mistake most brands make is launching WhatsApp broadcasts and calling that a strategy. The real win is when WhatsApp is triggered by behaviour — someone abandons a cart, someone browses a category, someone finishes a purchase. That's where the returns live. And when WhatsApp is working off the same data as email and Meta, you get compounding, not addition.

*(~40s. "Compounding, not addition" is the line.)*

---

# Q6. Results — how they compare, what drove them

Those numbers are outliers. A 63x ROI on WhatsApp isn't the average we see across our portfolio, and 70% of revenue coming from flows means the automation is genuinely carrying the business while the team focuses on the bigger plays.

What drove the results was discipline on the setup. We didn't try to launch everything at once. We built the flow architecture first, got the data clean, then turned WhatsApp on with real behavioural triggers. The channel mix was designed to compound, not compete. You can't fake that kind of result with tactics — it comes from the architecture.

*(~35s. Pair this with Ben's emotional reaction to the numbers.)*

# Q7. How do you explain a 63x ROI to a brand that's never seen it?

When a brand hasn't seen numbers like that, I start by saying it's not the platform on its own. It's a combination of a few things done well.

WhatsApp in SEA behaves differently from email in other regions. Open rates sit around 90%, not 30%. Customers treat it like a personal channel, which means the intent is higher the moment they opt in. That's the ceiling.

Most brands underperform on WhatsApp because they treat it like another email list. You broadcast, you get ignored, you conclude the channel doesn't work. Omnidesk had the opt-in quality, the segmentation, and the behavioural triggers in place before they pressed send. That's the whole difference.

*(~45s. This is your big moment — slow down, land "that's the whole difference".)*

# Q8. 70% revenue from flows — walk us through how they're built

The flow stack for Omnidesk covers the full lifecycle. Acquisition runs on email and WhatsApp with a welcome series that qualifies the customer early. Consideration picks up browse and abandoned cart across both channels, where WhatsApp usually closes the loop faster than email. Post-purchase moves into cross-sell and replenishment. Win-back catches lapsed segments before they go cold.

The way we think about automation in a mobile-first market is different. Email is still there, but WhatsApp often carries the emotional weight of the message, because that's where people are already reading.

The flow that outperformed our expectations was the WhatsApp welcome. We knew it would convert. We didn't expect it to convert that fast.

*(~50s. This is the longest answer — break it with the "WhatsApp welcome" beat.)*

# Q9. What does true omnichannel look like in practice?

The quick version: one profile, three channels, real time.

A customer browses a product page. That behaviour lands on their Klaviyo profile, which triggers a WhatsApp reminder two hours later. If they don't act, email picks it up the next day with a slightly different angle. Meanwhile they drop into a lookalike audience we send to Meta, so the paid side keeps momentum going.

Most brands tell themselves they're omnichannel because they're running three channels. Real omnichannel is when the channels talk to each other using the same customer data. That only works when the data sits in one place. That's the unlock.

*(~45s. "That's the unlock" is the landing.)*

# Q10. How does Klaviyo's pace of innovation factor into how you build?

Klaviyo's shipping velocity actually changes how we plan. We used to build strategies that had to hold up for 18 months. Now we're planning for six, knowing new capabilities will land that we'll want to fold in.

K:AI Customer Agent is the one I'm watching closely right now. For brands in SEA, being on a platform that moves this fast matters more than it does in the US or Europe — because the consumer behaviour here moves faster too. WhatsApp commerce didn't exist at this scale three years ago. Being on a platform that ships against that reality is a real advantage.

*(~40s. The "18 months to 6 months" line is the hook.)*

# Q11. How has this changed Omnidesk day-to-day?

A healthy brand-agency relationship runs on short feedback loops. With Omnidesk we're in the numbers weekly, planning sprints monthly, and the team on their side always knows what we're doing and why.

The way we work is to embed, not disappear. We build capability inside their team as we go, so the knowledge stays with them even if we step back. Nothing kills a CRM program faster than an agency that hoards the playbook.

*(~30s. "Embed, not disappear" is the line to remember.)*

# Q12. Advice to SEA brands on the fence about WhatsApp / omnichannel CRM

The hardest cost to see is the time you lose. Every quarter you spend on the wrong platform or running channels in silos is a quarter your competitors are compounding. That's the expensive mistake, not the software fee.

The brands we see hesitate the longest are usually the ones who got burned once and want to be sure. I get that. But the best way to get sure is to start small with one strong channel strategy, measure it properly, and expand when the numbers move. Don't wait for perfect data before you start. You'll be waiting forever.

*(~40s. End with "you'll be waiting forever" for a slight smile.)*

# Q13. What are you excited to build next?

What I'm watching across our portfolio is how AI changes the segmentation layer. Not flashy AI — the useful kind. Models that predict when a customer is about to lapse, or what product they're most likely to buy next.

The biggest opportunity in SEA over the next twelve months is going to be brands using AI to personalise WhatsApp at scale. The channel is already the best-performing one we have. If we can make it feel personal without making it expensive, that's the next level — for Omnidesk and for the market.

# ON-CAMERA PREP NOTES

**Three phrases to land, if nothing else:**
1. "One profile, three channels, real time."
2. "Compounding, not addition."
3. "Embed, not disappear."

**Things to avoid saying on camera:**
- "Leverage", "utilise", "robust", "best-in-class", "holistic"
- Any version of "at the end of the day"
- Stacking three adjectives ("fast, personal, and scalable")
- "Synergy" — Klaviyo will edit it out and you'll never live it down

**Pacing:**
- Breathe between sentences. On camera it always feels slower than it is.
- When a number lands (63x, 70%, 10%), pause for half a beat before the next sentence. Let it land.
- It's fine to say "Let me think about that for a second" on an unexpected question. It reads as considered, not hesitant.

**If you blank:**
- Fall back to the arc: bones were there → connected the channels → results compounded. Every answer can bend back to that.
